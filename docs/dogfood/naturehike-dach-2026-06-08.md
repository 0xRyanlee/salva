## Recommended Sequence

1. Build a channel-control map: current authorized sellers, grey-market sellers,
   territory rights, MAP/pricing, warranty owner, and inventory origin.
2. Approach one German sales/distribution partner and one Swiss distributor in
   parallel.
3. Prepare a retail-alliance pack: top 10 SKUs, landed margin, German product
   content, compliance, service SLA, launch calendar, and sell-through evidence.
4. Run a 90-day store/online pilot before granting exclusivity.

## Minimum Qualification Questions

For each distributor or agent:

- Which outdoor brands and conflicting tent/sleep brands are represented?
- Which named retailers and buying groups are active accounts?
- Annual sell-in, warehouse, field sales, service, and returns capability?
- Territory, exclusivity, minimum order, payment, currency, and Incoterms?
- Ability to provide German-language product data and EPR/compliance support?

For each alliance:

- Is onboarding central, member-led, or both?
- Required margin, rebates, data standard, listing fee, and marketing contribution?
- Online-only pilot possible?
- Who owns warranty and spare-part handling?

## Dogfood Findings

Agent-only research found 15 verified relevant candidates after three rounds and
covered all three countries and five channel types. Its 88.2% pooled recall is
relative to the post-hoc verified union, not external ground truth. Salva's first live snapshot
found two relevant specialist retailers, [Reimo](https://www.reimo.com/en/camping-shop)
and [Bergfreunde](https://www.bergfreunde.eu/camping), but no distributor,
agent, or alliance. Independent R2/R3 snapshots returned zero results due to DDG
HTML provider variability.

This does not prove Agent-only is generally superior. It proves the current Salva
configuration is not yet reliable for this channel-discovery task without:

1. stable SearXNG/frozen-corpus input plus provider-level error reporting;
2. a channel-type classifier and DACH-specific vocabulary/source packs.

## Risks Before Outreach

- Search presence is not proof that a company accepts new brands.
- Existing Naturehike listings may be marketplace or grey-market inventory.
- Retail alliances may require local distributor/service capability first.
- Exclusive contracts can block better partners if minimum performance is vague.
- EU/Swiss product compliance, packaging, EPR, warranty, and spare parts need
  separate legal/operational verification.

No outreach has been made; all candidates require commercial qualification.
